Patient Dossier #002

Medical Information

Donation ¥30,000
Status Surgery Completed

Story

Xiao Tu was born in 2009 in Sichuan Province with a Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD), a hole in the wall separating the heart's lower chambers. This condition caused abnormal blood flow between the ventricles, making his heart work much harder than normal and limiting his physical abilities.

As a secondary school student, Xiao Tu faced daily challenges. Simple activities like climbing stairs or participating in physical education classes left him breathless and exhausted. His condition not only affected his physical health but also his ability to fully engage in school life and social activities with his peers.

His family, struggling financially, had postponed the surgery for years, hoping his condition would improve on its own. However, as Xiao Tu grew older, the need for intervention became increasingly urgent.

Heart to Heart raised ¥30,000 to fund Xiao Tu's VSD repair surgery in 2024. The operation was a success, and Xiao Tu's recovery has been remarkable. He has returned to school with renewed energy and can now participate in activities that were previously impossible for him.

Xiao Tu's teachers report that his academic performance has improved significantly since the surgery, as he no longer struggles with the constant fatigue that once held him back. His story demonstrates how addressing congenital heart defects can transform not just physical health, but every aspect of a young person's life.

Impact

Xiao Tu's successful surgery has opened up a world of possibilities. He can now focus on his education and future aspirations without the constant burden of his heart condition. His family expresses deep gratitude to the Heart to Heart community for giving their son a second chance at a normal life.
